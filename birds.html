Understanding your bird’s relational needs

Birds are intriguing creatures that vary widely in size, behavior, and care needs. Many owners focus on food and habitat, but emotional well-being matters too. Unfortunately, birds can experience sadness and depression just like humans.

A number of root causes may contribute to depression, including physical illness, mental distress, losing a companion, or becoming increasingly bored. Identifying symptoms early can help improve well-being and lifespan.

Possible signs of depression

  • Reduced appetite
  • Becoming increasingly irritable
  • Aggressive behavior
  • Songs that sound different or more solemn
Be sure to tell your veterinarian if you notice any of these symptoms. Early recognition and treatment may significantly improve long-term health outcomes.

Ways to support bird well-being

Bird owners can improve a bird’s environment by providing enrichment, interaction, predictable routines, and stimulation that matches the species’ needs. If behavior changes suddenly, a consultation with a veterinarian or a Pexpert may help identify whether the issue is environmental or medical.
